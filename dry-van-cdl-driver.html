What makes a dry van job description work

Dry van is the most competitive segment in trucking recruitment. Hundreds of carriers are posting nearly identical jobs. The ones that get applications lead with home time and pay — that's what drivers care about first.

Most dry van postings bury the pay rate or leave it as "competitive." Drivers skip those. Put your CPM or hourly rate in the first sentence.

Key things to specify:

  • Drop-and-hook vs. live load — this matters more than most employers realize
  • Equipment year (2022+, late-model) — old trucks are a disqualifier for experienced drivers
  • Exact home time — "home weekly" can mean Friday evening or Sunday afternoon. Be specific.
  • No-touch status — a major selling point in dry van
  • MVR standards — vague language like "clean record" turns away drivers who may have one old ticket
