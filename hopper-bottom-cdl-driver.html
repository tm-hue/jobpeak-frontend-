Hopper bottom posting: what drivers look for

Hopper bottom is a niche segment with a smaller driver pool. Drivers who run hopper chose it for the pay premium and the specialized nature of the work. The key differentiator in hopper bottom postings is whether pneumatic training is provided — many experienced drivers transitioned from other freight and learned on the job. If you train, say it.

  • Tanker endorsement upfront — state it in the first line of requirements, not buried at the bottom.
  • Specify the commodity — grain, cement, fly ash, and plastic pellets each require different handling knowledge. "Dry bulk" tells a driver nothing specific.
  • Pneumatic training policy — if you provide it, lead with it. Opens your applicant pool significantly.
  • Seasonal vs year-round — agricultural hopper has seasonal peaks. Be honest about volume patterns so drivers know what to expect.
