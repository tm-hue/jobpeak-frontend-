What flatbed drivers look for in a job posting

Flatbed drivers chose flatbed over dry van — usually for the pay premium. If your posting doesn't mention tarping pay, they'll assume you don't pay it and move on immediately.

  • State tarping policy explicitly — per tarp, per load, or built into CPM? Say it. Ambiguity costs you applications.
  • Freight type specificity — steel coils, lumber, machinery, and oversized each require different skills. "Various flatbed freight" is a yellow flag to experienced drivers.
  • Flatbed premium — experienced flatbed drivers know they earn more than dry van. If you're paying dry van rates for flatbed work, fix that before you fix the job description.
  • Physical acknowledgment — flatbed is more demanding than dry van. Drivers who want flatbed aren't scared of this — they just want to know you understand what the job involves.
